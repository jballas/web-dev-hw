Old Fashioned Peanut Butter Cookies, ca 1953


Ingredients

  • Flour, 1/4 cups
  • Baking soda, 3/4 tsp
  • Baking powder, 1/2 tsp
  • Salt, 1/4 tsp
  • Shortening, 1/2 cup
  • Peanut butter, 1/2cup
  • Granulated sugar, 1/2 cup
  • Brown sugar, 1/2 cup
  • Egg, well beaten, 1

Directions

  1. Sift flour; add soda, baking powder and salt; sift again
  2. Cream shortening and peanut butter; gradually add the sugars and cream throughly. Add the well-beaten egg.
  3. Combine the dry ingredients and creamed mixture. Chill
  4. Form into balls 1 inch diameter and place on a lightly greased and floured baking sheet. Flatten with a fork dipped in flour making a crisscross pattern.
  5. Bake in moderate oven (375 ℉) 8 to 10 minutes. Makes 3/1/2 to 4 dozen cookies.

Alternative or Substitutions

Instead of shortening you can substitute butter or oil. Taste of Home explains you can use the same proportions for butter, just allow it to come to room temperature before you cream it with sugar. Better Homes and Gardens claims you can do a 1-1 substitute with vegetable oil. And according to Coconut Oil Online, you should use less coconut oil; only 3/4 cup for 1 cup of shortening.
